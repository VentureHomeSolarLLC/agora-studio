# Maryland Solar Incentives — Complete Guide

## Maryland Solar Access Program (MEA)

The Maryland Energy Administration (MEA) offers grants to income-eligible Maryland homeowners through the **Maryland Solar Access Program (MSAP)**.

Established by the Brighter Tomorrow Act of 2024, this program replaced the prior Residential Clean Energy Rebate.

### Grant Amount

- **Up to $7,500**
- Calculated at **$750 per kilowatt (kW)** of DC solar capacity installed

**Learn more:** [energy.maryland.gov](https://energy.maryland.gov)

---

## Maryland SREC Market

Maryland's Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS) requires utilities to source a percentage of electricity from solar.

### How It Works

- Solar owners earn **one SREC for every MWh** their system produces over the first **15 years**
- SRECs can be **sold to utilities** on the Maryland SREC market

### Current Value

Current Maryland SREC market prices are approximately **$50–55 per SREC** (the Solar Alternative Compliance Payment, or SACP, is set at $55 for 2025 and declines gradually to $22.50 by 2030).

### Example

An average 8 kW system producing 9 MWh/year can earn approximately **$450–500 annually** in SREC income, totaling an estimated **$3,500–4,000 over 15 years**.

### How to Participate

Venture Home registers your system in **PJM-EIS GATS** so your SRECs are created and available for sale.

- You can engage a **broker to sell your SRECs**
- Venture Home can point you to reputable options

## Property Tax Exemption

Maryland provides a **100% state and local real property tax exemption** for solar energy systems used to:
- Heat or cool a structure
- Provide hot water
- Generate electricity

This applies to both **residential and commercial properties**.

Your solar installation **will not increase your property tax assessment** — no action required.

### County Supplements

Certain counties offer **supplemental local property tax credits** (one-time credits, separate from the statewide exemption):

- **Anne Arundel County:** Up to $2,500 one-time property tax credit
- **Harford County:** Up to $2,500 per solar device, $5,000 per property per fiscal year
- **Prince George's County:** Up to $5,000 alternative energy tax credit (also available to lessees)

Contact your county tax authority or your Venture Home project manager for current program details and application requirements.

---

## Sales Tax Exemption

Maryland **exempts solar energy equipment** (panels, inverters, mounting, battery storage) and solar water heaters from state sales and use tax.

**Automatic at point of purchase** — on a $20,000 system, this saves approximately **$1,200** (6% rate).
