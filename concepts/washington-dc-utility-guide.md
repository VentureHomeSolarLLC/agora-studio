# Washington, D.C. Utility Guide — Pepco

## 1-to-1 Net Metering + Among the Highest SREC Values in the Country

### Net Metering Policy

Pepco provides **1-to-1 net metering** throughout DC.

Each kWh your system exports earns a kWh credit at the **full retail rate**. Credits roll forward month to month through December 31.

### Year-End True-Up

At year-end, credits **expire** — but Pepco pays out any remaining balance **greater than $25** at the generation-only rate (approximately **$0.088/kWh** as of early 2025, which excludes transmission costs).

This is **lower than retail** but ensures you're not simply losing credits.

### Fixed Charges

You will still receive a monthly **fixed charge (~$18)** that cannot be offset by solar credits.

## DC SRECs — Exceptional Value

DC has **one of the strongest SREC markets in the country**.

### Why So Valuable?

- DC's Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS) has:
  - **Aggressive solar carve-outs**
  - **High Alternative Compliance Payments** that utilities pay if they fall short
- This drives **high SREC demand and prices**

### Current Market

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **SREC Generation** | 1 per 1,000 kWh (1 MWh) |
| **Recent Prices** | ~$400/SREC |
| **Typical 8 kW System** | ~10 MWh/year = ~$4,000/year |
| **SREC Validity** | 3 years from generation |

### Example

A typical 8 kW system generating ~10 MWh/year earns approximately **$4,000/year in SREC income**.

### By Contrast

Maryland SRECs trade at roughly **$50–$60** — DC is **6-8x more valuable**.

### Registration

Venture Home registers all eligible DC customer-owned systems in the **DC SREC market**.

Once registered, SRECs are typically sold through a **broker who pays you directly**.

## Special Note for Maryland Border Areas

If you're a **Maryland customer served by Pepco** and your home is on DC feeder lines (common in border areas of Montgomery and Prince George's Counties), you may be eligible for **DC's SREC market**.

**Contact us to confirm** your eligibility.

## State Tax Incentives

DC does not currently offer a:
- State-level solar tax credit
- Battery-specific incentive

**The primary financial drivers in DC are:**
1. Net metering
2. The SREC program

Together these provide **exceptional economics** for solar customers.
