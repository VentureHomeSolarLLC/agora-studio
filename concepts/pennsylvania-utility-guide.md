# Pennsylvania Utility Guide — PECO, PPL, Duquesne Light, Met-Ed

## 1-to-1 Net Metering with Annual Cash-Out in May

### Net Metering Policy

Pennsylvania requires major utilities to provide **1-to-1 net metering** for full-service customers (those receiving both supply and distribution from the same utility).

Each kWh your system produces first offsets home consumption; any excess exported earns a kWh credit at the full retail rate, applied to your next bill.

- Credits roll forward month to month throughout the year without expiration — until the annual cash-out

---

## Annual Cash-Out (May Billing Cycle)

After the May billing cycle, Pennsylvania utilities are required by law to **cash out any remaining NEM credits**.

### Payout Rate

You will receive either a check or a credit to your account for the balance — but at the **"Price to Compare" rate**, which is approximately **half the retail rate** (supply + transmission, excluding distribution).

### Impact

This means credits that roll from summer into winter are **fully valuable**, but any leftover balance after May is paid out at a **significantly reduced rate**.

> 💡 **Plan accordingly** — the ideal scenario is consuming most of your accumulated credits before the May reset.

---

## ⚠️ Competitive Electric Suppliers — Important Warning

Pennsylvania utilities are **not required to provide net metering credits** if you receive electricity supply from a third-party Competitive Electric Supplier.

**If you switched your supply service and receive a separate supply bill**, your solar credits may not apply to those supply and transmission charges.

> 🚨 **If this applies to you, contact your distribution utility to return to full-service electricity** to maximize your net metering value.

## SRECs

Pennsylvania solar customers are eligible to earn and sell **Solar Renewable Energy Certificates (SRECs)** via the PJM-GATS registry.

Venture Home handles registration for all eligible customer-owned systems.

Each 1,000 kWh your system produces generates one SREC, which can be sold on the open market.

**Ask your project manager about current market pricing** and your options for monetizing PA SRECs.
