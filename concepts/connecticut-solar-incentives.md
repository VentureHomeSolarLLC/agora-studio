# Connecticut Solar Incentives — Complete Guide

## Energy Storage Solutions Program (CT Green Bank)

Connecticut's **Energy Storage Solutions (ESS) program**, administered by the CT Green Bank along with Eversource and United Illuminating, provides incentives for homeowners who install battery storage systems.

### Two Components

#### 1. Upfront Incentive

- **One-time payment** based on storage system's capacity (kWh)
- Standard residential systems can receive **up to $16,000**
- Paid through your installer within approximately **8 weeks** of project approval
- **Low-income customers** and those in underserved communities may qualify for higher incentive levels

#### 2. Performance Incentive

- **Ongoing payments for up to 10 years**
- Based on how much energy your battery contributes to the grid during active dispatch events
- Events typically: a few hours on high-demand summer and winter days
- Payments made **twice per year**

### How to Qualify

- Battery must be installed by a **participating contractor registered with the CT Green Bank**
- **Venture Home handles the ESS application on your behalf**

**Learn more:** [energystoragect.com](https://energystoragect.com)

## Property Tax Exemption

Connecticut requires that all **passive or active solar heating systems and geothermal energy resources be fully exempt from property taxes statewide** (CGS § 12-81(57)).

### What This Means

Your solar installation **will not increase your property tax assessment**.

**No application is required.**

---

## Sales Tax Exemption

Connecticut exempts **solar panel systems, battery storage, inverters, mounting equipment, and installation labor** from the 6.35% state sales and use tax.

### How It Works

- Exemption is **automatic at point of purchase**
- On a typical $20,000 system, this saves approximately **$1,300**

---

## Netting vs. Net Metering Note

Connecticut uses **"netting"** rather than traditional net metering. Excess generation is compensated as a **monetary credit** (not kWh) at a set rate lower than the full retail rate.

See [[net-metering|Understanding Net Metering]] for full explanation.
