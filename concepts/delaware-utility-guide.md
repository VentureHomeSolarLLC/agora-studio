# Delaware Utility Guide — Delmarva Power, Delaware Electric Cooperative

## 1-to-1 Net Metering with Annual True-Up + SREC Program

### Net Metering Policy

Delaware requires all major utilities — **Delmarva Power, Delaware Electric Cooperative, and DEMEC municipal members** — to provide **1-to-1 net metering** for residential systems.

- Credits are tracked in **kWh, not dollars**
- Each kWh your system produces that exceeds home consumption is **credited to your account**
- You draw credits back down when production is insufficient (nights, winter)

### Fixed Charges

Fixed monthly charges (meter fees) must be paid in cash and **cannot be offset by kWh credits**.

---

## Annual True-Up

Net metering credits reset at the end of your chosen **12-month period**.

### Unlike Some States

Delaware **does not pay out unused credits** — any remaining kWh credits simply **expire**.

### Choosing Your True-Up Month

- You can **choose your annual true-up month one time**
- You may **change it once after that**

---

## Green Energy Program (Upfront Rebates)

Delaware's Green Energy Program provides **upfront rebates** through participating utilities:

### Delmarva Power
- ~**$0.70/watt**, up to **$6,000**
- Application handled by Venture Home as part of installation

### Delaware Electric Cooperative
- **$0.50/watt** for first 5 kW
- **$0.20/watt** thereafter

> 💡 **Note:** Green Energy Program rebates are applied directly to your installation cost

---

## SREC Program

Delaware's **SREC Delaware** program allows customer-owned solar systems to earn and sell Solar Renewable Energy Certificates (SRECs) via an **annual auction process**.

### How It Works

- **One SREC** is generated for every 1,000 kWh (1 MWh) your system produces
- Submit **monthly production meter readings** through the SREC Delaware online portal (Venture Home provides setup guidance)
- Payments are on a **2-month delay** from production

### Pricing

| Years | Rate |
|-------|------|
| **1–10** | ~$30/SREC (auction-based) |
| **11–25** | $10/SREC flat rate |

### Example

An 8 kW system generating ~10 SRECs/year earns roughly **$300/year in years 1–10**.
