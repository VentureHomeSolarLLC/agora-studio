# Snow Guards: Protecting What's Below

When snow does slide off solar panels, it doesn't always do so gradually. Panels are slick and angled, and a large accumulated sheet of snow can **release all at once** — dropping a significant weight of snow and ice off the edge of your roof in a single event.

For many installations this isn't a concern. But if your panels are positioned above high-traffic areas, it's worth thinking about.

---

## When Snow Guards Matter

Snow guards are worth considering if any of the following apply:

**High-Priority Situations:**
- ✓ Panels are directly above your **front or back door**
- ✓ Panels overhang a **driveway or parking area** where vehicles park
- ✓ Panels are above a **deck, patio, or outdoor area** with regular foot traffic
- ✓ You have a **hot tub, outdoor furniture, or valuables** below the panels
- ✓ You've experienced rapid snow shedding that felt **unsafe or surprising**

---

## What Snow Guards Do

**Venture Home offers and installs snow guards** — small devices that attach to the panel racking.

**How they work:**
- Break up snow accumulation
- Cause snow to **release gradually** rather than all at once
- **Prevent the "avalanche" effect**

**What they don't do:**
- ❌ Prevent snow from clearing
- ❌ Reduce solar production
- ❌ Damage panels or racking

> 💡 Snow guards **control how snow comes off**, not whether it comes off.

## Installation

### New Installations

Snow guards can be **included in your original installation design** if your home's layout warrants them.

**Discuss with your project manager** during the design phase if you have concerns about snow shedding locations.

### Retrofitting

Already have solar but no snow guards? **They can be added to existing installations.**

- ✓ Straightforward installation
- ✓ Low-cost addition
- ✓ No disruption to system operation
