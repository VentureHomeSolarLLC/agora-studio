# Maine Utility Guide — CMP & Versant Power

## 1-to-1 Net Energy Billing (Full-Service Customers)

### Net Metering Policy

Maine's program is officially called **Net Energy Billing — Kilowatt-Hour Credit**.

Central Maine Power (CMP) and Versant Power are required under **Maine PUC Chapter 313 rules** to offer 1-to-1 net metering to solar customers who receive **full-service** (both supply and distribution) from the utility.

### How It Works

Net metering works by **subtracting your solar production from your grid consumption** in the same monthly billing cycle at the full retail rate.

- Each kWh you export **offsets one kWh** you consume
- Any **excess credits roll forward month to month for up to 12 months**
- **Unused credits after 12 months expire** — there is **no cash payout**

---

## Fixed Charges

Even with a solar system sized to cover 100% of your usage, **you will still receive a utility bill**.

**Fixed customer charges:** $24–$28/month

These cover grid infrastructure costs and **cannot be offset by solar credits**. Charged regardless of consumption.

---

## ⚠️ Competitive Electric Providers — Important Warning

Maine allows customers to choose their electricity supply from a **Competitive Electric Provider (CEP)** separate from CMP or Versant.

**If you receive two separate bills** — one from your distribution utility and one from your CEP — your supply charges **may not be credited** under net metering.

> 🚨 **If this applies to you, we strongly recommend switching back to full utility service** (supply + distribution from CMP or Versant) to ensure you receive full 1-to-1 net metering benefits.

**Contact your utility to make this change.**

Maine solar customers on customer-owned systems are eligible for **Class 1 NEPOOL-GIS RECs**.
