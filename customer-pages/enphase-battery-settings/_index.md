# Managing Your Enphase Battery: Settings, Modes, and Storm Guard

Your Enphase battery is managed entirely through the **Enphase App**, available on iOS and Android. Everything from your backup reserve level to your operating mode to Storm Guard can be adjusted in a few taps.

Here's a walkthrough of the key settings and what they mean.

---

## Finding Your Battery Settings

**Open the Enphase App** and:
1. Tap the **Battery icon** or navigate to **Devices > Battery**
2. You'll see your current state of charge, active operating mode, and quick access to settings
3. To adjust configuration, tap **Settings** or the **gear icon**

## Operating Modes

Enphase offers **three primary operating modes**. Your installer set a default at activation, but you can change this any time in the app.

### Savings Mode ⭐ (Recommended for Most Customers)

**This is the default mode for most Venture Home installations.**

**How it works:**
- Battery charges from solar during the day
- Battery discharges to power your home in the evening and overnight
- Reduces how much you draw from the grid
- **Backup reserve always protected** — won't be used for daily cycling

**Best for:** Maximizing energy savings while keeping backup power available

---

### Full Backup Mode

In Full Backup Mode, your battery prioritizes staying **fully charged at all times**.

**How it works:**
- Solar and grid power both contribute to keeping battery topped up
- Battery discharges **only during grid outage**
- Maximum preparedness for outages

**Trade-off:** Reduces daily energy savings because battery isn't cycling regularly

**Best for:**
- Extended periods of severe weather risk
- Medical equipment or critical loads requiring reliable backup
- Maximum peace of mind

---

### Time of Use (TOU) / Peak Shaving Mode

If your utility has **time-of-use rates** — where electricity costs more during peak hours (typically evenings).

**How it works:**
- Battery **charges during off-peak hours** (when power is cheaper)
- Battery **discharges during peak hours** (when power is most expensive)
- Takes advantage of rate differentials

**Best for:** Customers with significant utility rate differences between peak and off-peak

**Check:** Your utility rate schedule to determine if TOU mode makes sense

## Backup Reserve

Your **backup reserve** is the percentage of battery capacity held **exclusively for power outages**.

**Key point:** This energy is **never used for daily cycling** — it stays locked until the grid goes down.

### Recommended Setting

**Venture Home recommends: 10% to 20%**

This corresponds to a **depth of discharge of 80–90%**.

**Why this range?**
- ✓ Enough energy for most outage scenarios
- ✓ Still allows daily cycling for savings
- ✓ Optimal balance for most customers

### How to Adjust

1. Go to **Settings > Backup Reserve**
2. Use the **slider** to set your preferred percentage
3. App shows estimated **hours of backup capacity** based on your home's typical usage

### Setting Guidelines

| Reserve | Backup Duration | Daily Savings | Best For |
|---------|----------------|---------------|----------|
| **10%** | 1-2 hours | Maximum | Stable grid, prioritize savings |
| **20%** ⭐ | 2-4 hours | High | **Recommended balance** |
| **50%** | 6-10 hours | Reduced | Frequent outages |
| **100%** | 12-24 hours | Minimal | Storm mode, medical needs |

**Too high (50%+):**
- Less battery available for daily use
- Reduced savings
- Only recommended for frequent outage areas

**Too low (5%):**
- Very little backup capacity
- Risk of empty battery during unexpected outage

## Storm Guard

**Storm Guard** is one of the most valuable features in the Enphase App.

### What It Does

When enabled, Storm Guard **monitors weather forecasts** in your area. If severe weather is detected — hurricanes, tropical storms, major nor'easters, or other significant events — your system will **automatically pre-charge your battery to 100%** before the storm arrives.

**Even if that means drawing power from the grid to do so.**

### Why It Matters

When the storm hits and the grid goes down, you're starting from a **full battery** rather than whatever state of charge was left over from normal daily cycling.

**Maximum backup power when you need it most.**

### How to Enable

1. Go to **Settings > Storm Guard**
2. **Toggle it ON**
3. Set threshold for weather events (or use default)

### Recommended Settings

**We recommend leaving Storm Guard ON year-round because:**
- ✅ Only activates when warranted
- ✅ No downside to having full battery before storm
- ✅ Fully automatic — no action needed from you
- ✅ Provides peace of mind during severe weather seasons
