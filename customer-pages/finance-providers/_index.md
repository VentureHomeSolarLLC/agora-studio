# Your Finance Provider: Billing, Contacts, and Home Sale/Refinance Guide

Your solar system was financed through one of several providers Venture Home works with. This article explains how to identify your provider, who to contact for billing and account questions, and what steps to take if you're refinancing your home or planning to sell.

## How to Identify Your Finance Provider

Your finance provider is listed in the agreement you signed when you went solar. If you're unsure, check any billing statements or emails you received after installation. You can also contact our Customer Success team and we'll help point you in the right direction.

---

If you financed your system with a **loan**, you own the solar equipment. Your monthly payment goes to your lender. Service, warranties, and system support are handled separately through Venture Home.

## Leases and Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs)

If you signed a **lease or PPA**, the finance company owns the solar equipment on your roof. You pay a monthly fee — either for the system itself (lease) or for the energy it produces (PPA). Because the equipment belongs to a third party, there are specific rules around selling, refinancing, and service.

**One important benefit of TPO agreements:** because the company owns the system, monitoring, maintenance, and repairs are included at no additional cost to you for the life of the agreement. You never pay out of pocket if something breaks.

**Refinancing:** Use the SunStrong support portal, which includes a dedicated refinancing request form.

### Palmetto LightReach

- **Website:** golightreach.com / palmetto.com
- **App:** Palmetto App (available in the App Store and Google Play)
- **Customer service:** Refer to your billing statement for your direct contact number, or log into your Palmetto account.

**Service coverage:**
- 24/7 proactive monitoring included — Palmetto alerts you if an issue is detected
- All maintenance and repairs included at no cost
- 25-year extended hardware warranties
- 90% production guarantee for the full 25-year term — if your system falls short, Palmetto compensates you for the difference

**Selling your home:** The LightReach plan can be **transferred to the new buyer**, subject to credit approval and a signed transfer agreement. You may also have the option to **buy out the system** — contact Palmetto for terms. Start this process early, as transfer paperwork takes time.

**Refinancing:** Contact Palmetto/LightReach directly — your mortgage lender may need a consent or subordination letter from them.

### Enfin (by Qcells)

- **Phone:** (833) 586-3677
- **Hours:** Mon–Fri 5am–9pm PT | Sat–Sun 7am–4pm PT
- **Website:** enfin.com

**Service coverage:**
- 24/7 monitoring by Omnidian (Enfin's dedicated third-party monitoring partner)
- All maintenance and repairs included at no cost for the full 25-year term
- Battery and inverter replacements included if needed during the agreement term
- Production guarantee with per-kWh compensation paid to you for any underproduction — specific guarantee amounts are in your agreement

**Selling your home:** You have three options:
1. **Transfer** the agreement to the new buyer
2. **Buy out** the system (available after 5 years in service)
3. **Prepay** the remaining monthly payments in full

Contact Enfin at least **30 days before closing** to begin the process.

**Refinancing:** Contact Enfin directly — your lender will likely need a letter related to the UCC-1 filing on the equipment.
