# Battery Backup Guide

Your battery is more than just a backup power source — it's a tool that can reduce your electric bill, protect your home during outages, and give you control over your energy.

This guide covers everything from initial setup to advanced features.

## What Your Battery Can Do

### During Normal Operation
- Store excess solar energy for use at night
- Reduce your electric bill by avoiding peak utility rates
- Provide power during brief grid fluctuations

### During Outages
- Automatically power your backed-up circuits
- Recharge from solar during daylight
- Keep essentials running for hours or days

## Settings & Features

### Operating Modes

**Savings Mode (Recommended)**
Your battery charges from solar during the day and powers your home in the evening. Your backup reserve is always protected for outages.

**Full Backup Mode**
Battery stays fully charged at all times, only discharging during outages. Best for storm seasons or if you have critical medical equipment.

**Time-of-Use Mode**
If your utility has variable rates, this mode optimizes charging and discharging to save you the most money.

### Storm Preparation

[[storm-guard|Storm Guard]] (Enphase) and [[storm-watch|Storm Watch]] (Tesla) are automatic features that monitor weather forecasts. When severe weather is detected, your battery will pre-charge to 100% — ensuring you have maximum backup capacity when the storm hits.

**Enable it:** Settings > Storm Guard (or Storm Watch) > Toggle ON

## Understanding Your Battery

### What Gets Powered During an Outage?

Most battery installations back up a dedicated set of circuits, not your entire home:

✅ **Typically backed up:**
- Lights in main living areas
- Refrigerator and freezer
- Internet router and phone chargers
- A few outlets for essentials

❌ **Typically not backed up:**
- Central air conditioning (high power draw)
- Electric dryers
- Pool pumps
- Electric vehicle chargers

Want to change what's backed up? Contact our team — some adjustments are possible.

### How Long Will It Last?

This depends on three things:

1. **Your battery size** (13.5 kWh Powerwall, 10 kWh Enphase, etc.)
2. **Your backup reserve setting** (we recommend 20%)
3. **What you're running** (fridge vs. air conditioning makes a big difference)

**Typical durations:**
- Essential circuits only: 12-24 hours
- Including AC or heat: 4-8 hours
- With solar recharging on sunny days: Indefinite during the day

See [[battery-duration|Battery Duration Calculator]] for personalized estimates.
