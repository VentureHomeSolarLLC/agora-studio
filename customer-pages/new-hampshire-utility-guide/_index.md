# New Hampshire Utility Guide — Eversource & Liberty Utilities

## Alternative Net Metering 2.0

New Hampshire's current program, in place since 2017, is the **Alternative Net Metering Tariff**.

It works in **two stages** within each monthly billing cycle.

### Stage 1: Monthly Netting (Within Same Billing Period)

Solar production that offsets grid consumption in the same month nets **nearly 1-to-1**.

You only pay a **small non-bypassable charge of 1–2 cents per kWh** on the portion offset — these support public benefit programs and cannot be avoided.

**Example:** If you use 700 kWh from the grid and produce 700 kWh of solar, you pay only:
- $7–$14 in non-bypassable charges
- Plus your fixed customer charge ($14–$20/month)

### Stage 2: Monthly Excess Production (Rollover Credits)

If your solar production exceeds your consumption in a month, the surplus is credited at a **reduced rate**:

**Reduced rate =** Default Energy Service (supply) + Transmission + 25% of Distribution

This is **meaningfully less than the full retail rate**.

**Example:** If you produce 700 kWh but only use 500 kWh:
- First 500 kWh net at nearly retail
- Excess 200 kWh rolls over at reduced rate

These credits roll forward **indefinitely with no expiration**.

### Real-World Impact

For a system sized to 100% of your annual usage, only about **10% of annual production** receives the lower rollover credit rate.

The **vast majority of production nets at the higher in-period rate**.

## ⚠️ Competitive Electric Suppliers — Important Warning

New Hampshire utilities are **not required to provide net metering credits** for customers who receive electricity supply from a Competitive Electric Supplier.

**If you switched your supply to a third-party provider**, your solar credits may not apply to those supply charges.

> 🚨 **If this applies to you, contact your utility to return to standard full-service electricity** to ensure your system provides full net metering value.
