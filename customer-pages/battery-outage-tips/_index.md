# Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Battery During an Outage

## Before the Outage

### ✅ Enable Storm Watch / Storm Guard

Make sure **Storm Watch** (Tesla) or **Storm Guard** (Enphase) is enabled in your app **before severe weather arrives**.

**What it does:** Pre-charges your battery to **100% automatically** when a weather alert is issued for your area.

**How to enable:**
- **Tesla:** Powerwall > Settings > Storm Watch
- **Enphase:** Settings > Storm Guard

See our battery settings articles for full instructions:
- [[tesla-powerwall-settings|Managing Your Tesla Powerwall]]
- [[enphase-battery-settings|Managing Your Enphase Battery]]

---

## During the Outage

### ✅ Reduce High-Draw Loads

During an extended outage, **avoid running** on battery power unless necessary:
- ❌ Central air conditioning
- ❌ Electric dryers
- ❌ Electric water heaters
- ❌ Other high-consumption appliances

**Why:** These can drain your battery in hours instead of days.

---

### ✅ Let the Sun Work for You

On sunny days during an outage:
- ☀️ Your solar panels **actively recharge** your battery
- ☀️ The more you shift high-draw activity to **daylight hours**, the more you preserve your overnight reserve

**Strategy:**
- Run dishwasher, laundry, etc. during sunny periods
- Charge devices during the day
- Conserve battery for nighttime when solar isn't producing
