# Monitoring Your Solar System by Manufacturer

Your solar system can be monitored through a manufacturer-specific app that gives you real-time and historical performance data. This guide covers how to access and interpret monitoring for each major system type.

---

## Enphase Systems

### Getting Started

**Download:** Enphase App (App Store or Google Play)

**Account Setup:**
- Create an Enphase account or log in if one was set up during installation
- Not sure if you have an account? Contact Customer Success and we'll help you get set up

### What You'll See

The home screen gives you a **real-time snapshot** of your system:
- Current power production
- Home energy consumption
- Battery status (if equipped) — charge level and charging/discharging state

The **energy flow diagram** shows the relationship between your solar panels, battery, home, and grid at any given moment.

**Production History:** View by day, week, month, or year. Useful for tracking seasonal trends and comparing current production to previous periods.

### If You Have an Enphase Battery

Full visibility into:
- Battery state of charge
- How it's being used
- Backup mode settings
- **Adjustable settings:** Reserve percentage for outages (directly in app)

### What Does Normal Look Like?

**Clear day:** Production graph shows a smooth **bell curve**:
- Zero before sunrise
- Climbing through morning
- Peaking early afternoon
- Tapering to zero by sunset

**Shorter, flatter curves** are normal in winter or on cloudy days.

**Red flags:** Flat lines during daylight, sudden drops, or days with zero production.

## SolarEdge Systems

### Getting Started

**Download:** mySolarEdge App (App Store or Google Play)

**Account Setup:**
- Log in with your SolarEdge account credentials
- Need help? Contact Customer Success

### What You'll See

**Dashboard shows:**
- Current power output
- Today's total energy production
- Lifetime generation

**Panel-Level Data:** SolarEdge's standout feature — see exactly how **each panel** is performing. Makes it easy to spot if a single panel is underperforming due to shading or another issue.

### What Does Normal Look Like?

**Production:** Bell curve on sunny days, peaking early afternoon, zero overnight.

**Panel-Level View:** All panels should show similar output levels. If one panel is consistently significantly lower, that's worth flagging.

## Tesla Systems

### Getting Started

**Download:** Tesla App (App Store or Google Play)

**Account Setup:**
- Log in with your Tesla account
- Your solar system appears under your home's energy products
- Don't see it? Contact Customer Success

### What You'll See

**Energy Tab:** Real-time view of your home's energy flow showing:
- Solar production
- Home consumption
- Grid usage
- Battery status (if equipped)

**Tap into each category** for more detail. View history by day, week, month, or year.

### If You Have a Tesla Powerwall

Full control over:
- **Backup reserve percentage**
- **Operating mode:**
  - Self-powered
  - Time-based control
  - Backup-only
- **Storm Watch activation**
- Battery charge level and daily charge/discharge tracking

### What Does Normal Look Like?

**Production:** Smooth bell curve on sunny days.

**Energy Flow:** Healthy system shows:
- **Day:** Energy flowing from solar → home and battery
- **Night:** Energy flowing from battery/grid → home

SunPower customers may use one of three apps depending on system age and configuration:

Newer SunPower systems use the **SunStrong App** (introduced as part of SunPower's transition to SunStrong as service provider).

Regardless of app:
- **Bell curve on sunny days** — rising morning, peaking early afternoon, zero by sunset
- **Consistent daily production** with no gaps or flat lines during daylight

## Quick Reference: Normal Production Patterns

| Condition | What to Expect |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Sunny day** | Smooth bell curve, peaks early afternoon |
| **Partly cloudy** | Bell curve with dips when clouds pass |
| **Overcast** | Lower, flatter curve but still producing |
| **Winter** | Shorter curve (fewer daylight hours), lower peak |
| **Night** | Zero production (this is normal!) |

## Red Flags to Watch For

Contact Customer Success if you see:
- ❌ Flat line during daylight hours
- ❌ Sudden drop to zero that doesn't recover
- ❌ Multiple days with zero production
- ❌ One panel significantly lower than others (SolarEdge systems)
- ❌ Error codes or alerts in your app
