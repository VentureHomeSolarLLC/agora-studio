# New York Utility Guide — Con Edison, NYSEG, Central Hudson, National Grid

## 1-to-1 Net Metering with Customer Benefit Contribution Charge

### Net Metering Policy

New York requires all utilities to provide **1-to-1 net metering**.

Each kWh exported earns a credit that offsets future consumption at the **full retail rate**.

- Excess credits roll forward for up to **20 years** — there is no annual true-up or cash-out for most customers

### Third-Party Suppliers

Third-party electricity suppliers in New York are **required to credit supply charges 1-to-1 as well**, so switching suppliers does not jeopardize your net metering.

---

## Customer Benefit Contribution (CBC) Charge

For systems installed after 2021, a monthly **Customer Benefit Contribution charge** applies.

### What It Is

- Charge is based on your solar system's **nameplate DC capacity (in kW)**, not your production or consumption
- **Partially or fully offset** by your net metering credits in most months

### Example

An 8 kW DC system served by National Grid would pay **$7.76/month** in CBC, in addition to the standard Basic Service Charge ($16–$24/month).

## NYS Solar & Storage Tax Credit

New York residents who install solar on their primary residence may be eligible for the **NYS Solar Tax Credit**, worth **25% of qualified solar equipment costs up to $5,000**.

### For Purchased Systems

- File **Form IT-255** with your New York State personal income tax return
- Credit can carry forward up to 5 years

### For Leased Systems

- Claim **100% of your annual lease payments** each year until the $5,000 total cap is reached

> ⚠️ Consult a tax professional to confirm eligibility. We cannot provide tax advice.

## Battery Incentives — Con Edison DLM Program

Con Edison customers with battery storage can enroll in the **Distributed Load Management (DLM)** program, which pays for battery dispatch during peak grid demand.

Contact our team or check Con Edison's website for current enrollment and incentive details.
