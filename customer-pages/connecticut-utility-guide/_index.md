# Connecticut Utility Guide — Eversource & UI

## Net Metering: Residential Renewable Energy Solutions (RRES) Program

### Net Metering Policy

Connecticut's current solar program, **Residential Renewable Energy Solutions (RRES)**, offers two options:
- **Netting Tariff** (nearly all Venture Home customers)
- Buy-All

### Netting Tariff: How It Works

**1-to-1 net metering:** Every kWh your solar system exports is credited against what you consume from the grid at the same retail rate.

- **No annual true-up** — excess credits roll forward month to month and year to year with **no expiration**

### Solar Production Charge (Solar Energy Adjustment)

The key difference from older net metering programs is the **Solar Production Charge**, called the **Solar Energy Adjustment** on Eversource bills.

**What it is:** Charge based on your system's **total solar production**, measured by a production meter.

**Rate:** Depends on the year your RRES application was submitted — **locked in for 20 years**.

**Example:** A system producing 600 kWh in a month with a 2026 application date would be charged approximately **$24 that month** in Solar Production Charge — in addition to any remaining grid usage charges.

## Your Monthly Bill

Your bill will typically include:

| Charge | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Fixed monthly charge** | $9–$13 | Cannot be offset |
| **Solar Production Charge** | Varies | Based on total production |
| **Delivery charges** | Varies | If you consumed more than produced |
| **Net Supply Credit** | Negative | If you produced more than consumed |

**$0 bill possible** when credits offset all charges including fixed and production charges.

**Seasonal pattern:** Many CT customers generate excess credits spring through fall, then draw them down in winter. This is **expected and normal**.

## Energy Storage Incentives (ESS Program)

The **Connecticut Energy Storage Solutions (ESS) Program**, administered by the CT Green Bank with Eversource and UI, offers two incentive tracks for battery customers.

### Passive Dispatch — Upfront Incentive

- **Upfront discount** for agreeing to 10 years of mandatory summer participation
- Battery automatically discharged on scheduled weekday events June–August, 5–8 PM
- **Current rate:** $212.50/kWh (standard customers, late 2025)
- **Example:** 13.5 kWh Powerwall 3 = approximately **$2,868 upfront**
- **Mandatory participation** — clawback penalties for underperformance

### Active Dispatch — Performance-Based Incentive

- **Paid twice annually** based on average kW performance during optional dispatch events
- Events: up to ~60 summer, ~5 winter
- **No upfront discount**

| Years | Summer Rate | Winter Rate |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 1–5 | $200/kW | $25/kW |
| 6–10 | $115/kW | $15/kW |

**Example:** 3 kW average performance = **$675/year in years 1–5**

### Enrollment Options

Customers can enroll in:
- Both Passive and Active
- Active only
- Neither

**Venture Home customers** can discuss enrollment options with our team.
