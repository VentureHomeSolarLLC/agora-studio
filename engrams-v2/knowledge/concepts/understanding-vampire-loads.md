# Understanding Vampire Loads

Vampire loads refer to the electricity consumed by electronic devices while they are turned off or in standby mode but still plugged in. Common examples include TVs, computer chargers, and coffee makers. These loads can add up to a significant portion of a household's energy consumption, impacting both energy bills and battery life during power outages.
