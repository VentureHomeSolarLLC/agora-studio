# Adding a Battery to Your Existing Solar System

Yes — and it's one of the most common upgrades Venture Home customers make. A battery lets you store the energy your panels produce and use it when the grid goes down, when rates are highest, or overnight. Here's what you need to know.

## Is My System Compatible?

Compatibility depends on your **inverter type**, not just your panels.

**Enphase systems** are the most flexible. You can add either an Enphase IQ Battery or a Tesla Powerwall to an existing Enphase system. Both integrate cleanly with Enphase's microinverter architecture.

**Tesla systems** can only be paired with a Tesla Powerwall. You cannot add an Enphase battery to a Tesla inverter system.

**SolarEdge and other inverter systems** — compatibility depends on the specific configuration. Contact our team and we'll assess your system.

**Lease or PPA customers:** Your finance provider may need to approve the addition before we can proceed. This is case-by-case, so reach out to us and we'll help navigate it. Cash and loan customers can generally move forward without that step.

## What Does the Installation Involve?

Battery add-ons follow a similar process to a new solar installation:

### Permitting and Utility Approval
Adding a battery requires its own permit and, in most cases, an updated interconnection filing with your utility. This is the same approval process as your original solar system.

### Timeline
Plan for a similar timeline to solar — typically several weeks from signed agreement to activation, depending on your municipality and utility.

### Site Visit
Existing Venture Home customers often don't need a full site visit. In many cases we can assess your system and design the addition remotely using your existing system data.

## How Is a Battery Sized?

There's no one-size-fits-all answer. The right battery size depends on two things: what you want to back up, and how large your solar system is.

If you want whole-home backup, you'll need more capacity than if you're just protecting critical circuits like lights, the refrigerator, and your router. We'll design the system around your specific goals. When you contact us, it helps to think about what you'd want powered during an outage — that conversation drives the recommendation.

## What Does It Cost, and Are There Incentives?

Venture Home offers batteries through a **prepaid lease structure**, which allows the tax credit value under federal clean energy incentives to be passed through to you as a reduction in the upfront price. This can meaningfully reduce the cost of the battery — often making it comparable to or less expensive than a whole-home generator, with the added benefit of solar charging, no fuel costs, and no maintenance.

On top of the federal passthrough, customers in Massachusetts, Rhode Island, Connecticut, and New York have access to state-level battery incentives that make adding a battery even more affordable. These programs change periodically, so contact our team for current figures when you're ready to explore.
