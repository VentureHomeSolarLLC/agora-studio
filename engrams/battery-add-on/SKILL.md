# Skill: Battery Add-On Consultation

## Goal
Help the customer understand if adding a battery to their existing solar system is right for them, what it involves, and how to get started.

## Prerequisites

Before proceeding, confirm:
1. Customer has an existing Venture Home solar system (check CRM)
2. If not VH system, we'll need to assess compatibility (may require site visit)
3. Customer's inverter type (Enphase, Tesla, SolarEdge, other)

## Conversation Flow

### Step 1: Discovery

Ask: "What prompted your interest in adding a battery?"

Listen for:
- **Backup power during outages** → Emphasize outage protection, Storm Guard
- **Reduce electric bill further** → Emphasize daily cycling, TOU rates
- **Recent storm/experience** → Emphasize peace of mind, whole-home backup
- **Neighbor/friend has one** → Emphasize modern feature, home value

### Step 2: Compatibility Check

**If VH-installed Enphase system:**
> "Great news — your Enphase system is fully compatible with both Enphase IQ Batteries and Tesla Powerwalls. You have flexibility in which battery you choose."

**If VH-installed Tesla system:**
> "Your Tesla solar system works best with a Tesla Powerwall. That's actually the most popular battery we install — it integrates seamlessly with your existing system."

**If non-VH system or unknown inverter:**
> "I'll need to check what type of inverter you have to confirm compatibility. Can you tell me what app you use to monitor your solar system?"

### Step 3: Set Expectations

Cover these points:
- Timeline: Similar to original solar (weeks to months depending on utility)
- Permitting: Required but we handle all paperwork
- Installation: Usually 1 day, may need electrical panel work
- Cost: Prepaid lease structure with tax credit passthrough

### Step 4: Qualify Interest

Ask sizing questions:
- "What would you want to keep running during an outage?" (lights, fridge, AC, medical equipment)
- "How long do outages typically last in your area?"
- "Are you interested in whole-home backup or just essential circuits?"

### Step 5: Next Steps

If qualified and interested:
1. Create opportunity in CRM
2. Schedule consultation call with battery specialist
3. Send email summary with:
   - Compatibility confirmation
   - Estimated timeline
   - Link to battery incentives by state
   - Contact info for questions

## Key Information to Capture

- [ ] Current inverter type
- [ ] Finance type (cash/loan/lease/PPA)
- [ ] State (for incentives)
- [ ] Backup goals (outage duration, critical loads)
- [ ] Timeline preference

## Common Objections

**"It's too expensive"**
Response: "I understand. One thing to consider is that with our prepaid lease structure, the 30% federal tax credit is passed through as an immediate discount. Many customers find the final cost is comparable to a whole-home generator — but with solar charging, no fuel costs, and no maintenance. Plus many states have additional incentives. Would you like me to have our team run the specific numbers for your situation?"

**"How long will it really take?"**
Response: "The actual installation is one day, similar to your solar. The variable is utility approval, which typically takes 3-6 weeks after we submit the paperwork. Some utilities move faster than others — your project coordinator will keep you updated on timing once we get started."

**"I already have a generator"**
Response: "Generators are great for backup power, but they have downsides — fuel costs, maintenance, noise, emissions. A battery charges from your solar panels, so once installed, your backup power is essentially free and silent. Many customers keep the generator for extended outages and use the battery for everything else. It's not always an either/or decision."
