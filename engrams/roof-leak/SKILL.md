# Skill: Roof Leak Response

## Goal
Handle reports of roof leaks near solar installations, schedule timely inspections, determine if leak is installation-related, and coordinate repairs under warranty or refer to roofing contractors.

## Priority Level
**HIGH** — Schedule inspection within 1-3 business days

Water damage can worsen quickly and cause secondary damage to structure, insulation, and electrical systems.

## Information Gathering

### Critical Questions

**Ask in order:**

1. **"Are you experiencing active leaking right now, or was this a past event?"**
   - Active leak = higher priority
   - Past event = still important, but less urgent

2. **"Where exactly are you seeing water?"**
   - Ceiling stain location
   - Active dripping
   - Attic moisture
   - Wall damage

3. **"When did you first notice the leak?"**
   - Date and time
   - Weather conditions at the time

4. **"Is the leak near your solar panels, or elsewhere on the roof?"**
   - Near panels = solar-related possible
   - Away from panels = likely unrelated, but still inspect

5. **"Do you have any photos of the leak or water damage?"**
   - Request photos immediately
   - Both interior damage and roof exterior if safe

### Document

- [ ] Leak location (interior and approximate roof location)
- [ ] When leak started
- [ ] Weather conditions when noticed
- [ ] Any temporary measures taken
- [ ] Photos provided
- [ ] System type and installation date
- [ ] Customer-owned or Lease/PPA

## Initial Response

### Set Expectations

> "I'm scheduling a roof inspection for you. A technician will examine all the solar-related roof penetrations and determine whether the leak is related to our installation work. This inspection is typically completed within 1-3 business days."

### Emphasize Importance

> "Even if you're not certain the leak is related to the solar installation, it's important we inspect it quickly. Water damage can worsen and affect your home's structure, insulation, and electrical systems. We'd rather rule out our work than have you dealing with a worsening situation."

### Safety Warning

> "Please don't attempt to access the roof or inspect the panels yourself. We want our trained technician to assess it safely. In the meantime, you can place buckets to catch any dripping water and move valuables away from the affected area."

## Scheduling the Inspection

### Gather Details

**For scheduling:**
- Preferred days/times
- Adult availability (required for inspection)
- Access to attic if needed
- Pets or other considerations
- Gate codes or access restrictions

**What to tell customer:**
> "The technician will need access to your attic if possible, and will examine both the interior (where you're seeing water) and the roof exterior. The inspection typically takes 1-2 hours. They'll call you [when] to confirm the appointment."

## Inspection Process

### What Technician Will Do

**Roof Exterior:**
- Examine all solar mounting penetrations
- Check flashing integrity around mounts
- Look for gaps, cracks, or sealant failures
- Document with photos
- Check surrounding roof condition

**Interior/Attic:**
- Trace water intrusion path
- Check for moisture in insulation
- Look for structural damage
- Identify entry point
- Document with photos

**Determination:**
- Assess if leak path originates at solar penetrations
- Evaluate if flashing/sealing is compromised
- Rule out or confirm installation-related cause

### Possible Findings

**Solar-Related (Warranty Covered):**
- Improper flashing installation
- Sealant failure
- Installation damage to roofing material
- Mounting point gaps

**Non-Solar Related:**
- Aging roofing material
- Failed non-solar flashing
- Storm damage to non-solar areas
- Gutter/ drainage issues
- Pre-existing conditions

**Indeterminate:**
- Multiple potential sources
- Requires roofing contractor assessment
- Needs weather testing

## Communication After Inspection

### If Solar-Related (Warranty)

> "Our technician has determined that the leak is related to the solar installation and is covered under our 10-year workmanship warranty. We're scheduling repairs at no cost to you. The repair will include [specific work] and should be completed within [timeframe]."

**Explain:**
- What caused the leak
- What repairs are needed
- Timeline for completion
- Warranty coverage confirmation
- Any temporary measures until repair

### If NOT Solar-Related

> "Our technician completed the inspection and determined that the leak is not related to the solar installation. The leak appears to be caused by [specific cause]. While this isn't covered under our warranty, we wanted to rule out our work so you can proceed with the appropriate repairs. We've provided you with a detailed report you can share with your roofing contractor."

**Provide:**
- Detailed inspection report
- Photos of findings
- Documentation of non-solar cause
- Recommended next steps
- Referral to roofing contractors (if available)

### If Unclear

> "The inspection revealed multiple potential sources for the leak. While we didn't find clear evidence that the solar installation is the cause, we recommend having a qualified roofing contractor perform additional testing to definitively determine the source. We've provided you with our findings to share with them."

**Provide:**
- Inspection findings
- Areas ruled out
- Areas needing further assessment
- Recommended roofing contractors

## Lease/PPA Customers

### Key Difference

Finance provider may be responsible for installation-related leaks.

### Process

1. **Inspect as normal** — determine cause
2. **If solar-related:**
   - Contact finance provider
   - Provide inspection report
   - Coordinate repair approval
   - Schedule repair (we or their vendor)
3. **Keep customer informed** throughout

### What to Tell Customer

> "Since you have a [lease/PPA], your finance provider may be responsible for any installation-related repairs. We'll inspect and determine the cause, then coordinate with them on the repair if it's related to the solar work. You won't pay for warranty-covered repairs."

## Warranty Documentation

### If Covered

**Documentation required:**
- Inspection report with findings
- Photos of leak source
- Explanation of installation-related cause
- Repair plan and cost estimate
- Timeline for completion

### If Not Covered

**Documentation provided:**
- Inspection report
- Photos showing non-solar cause
- Description of findings
- Recommended next steps
- Roofing contractor referrals (optional)

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Clear Solar-Related Leak

**Signs:**
- Water path directly from mounting point
- Failed flashing visible
- Recent installation (within 10 years)

**Action:**
- Confirm warranty coverage
- Schedule immediate repair
- Document for warranty file
- Customer pays nothing

### Scenario 2: Aging Roof, Not Solar-Related

**Signs:**
- Roof 15+ years old
- Multiple leak areas
- Deteriorated shingles away from panels
- Solar mounts intact and sealed

**Action:**
- Provide detailed report
- Recommend roofing contractor
- Offer to re-inspect after roof repair
- Not warranty-covered

### Scenario 3: Storm Damage

**Signs:**
- Recent severe weather
- Visible damage to roof and panels
- Multiple homes in area affected

**Action:**
- Treat as storm damage Engram
- Document for insurance
- Coordinate with insurance adjuster
- May be insurance claim, not warranty

### Scenario 4: Indeterminate Source

**Signs:**
- Water path unclear
- Multiple potential entry points
- Requires weather to test

**Action:**
- Document all findings
- Recommend roofing contractor for further testing
- Offer to re-inspect if needed
- Provide detailed report

## Follow-Up

### After Repair (If Warranty)

- Confirm repair completed
- Test during next rain
- Follow up in 30 days
- Warranty file updated
- Close ticket

### After Referral (If Not Warranty)

- Check in after 2 weeks
- Offer re-inspection if roof repaired
- Ensure customer has resources
- Document outcome

## Escalation Triggers

**Escalate immediately:**
- Severe water damage (ceiling collapse, electrical hazard)
- Customer extremely distressed
- Multiple failed repair attempts
- Dispute over warranty coverage
- Insurance company needs technical testimony

**Escalate to:**
- Field operations (scheduling priority)
- Warranty team (coverage disputes)
- Management (customer satisfaction)
- Legal (if needed, rare)

## Key Phrases

**Urgency without panic:**
> "We want to get someone out quickly to assess this. Roof leaks can cause damage if left unaddressed, so let's get it inspected within the next few days."

**Setting expectations:**
> "The technician will examine all the solar mounting points and trace the water path to determine if it's related to the installation. This inspection typically takes 1-2 hours."

**Warranty confirmation:**
> "If the leak is related to our installation work, it's covered under our 10-year workmanship warranty and we'll repair it at no cost to you."

**Non-warranty (tactful):**
> "The inspection determined the leak is coming from [specific cause unrelated to solar]. While this isn't covered under our warranty, we wanted to give you a clear answer so you can get it properly repaired."
