# What if I Have a Roof Leak Near My Solar Panels?

If you notice a roof leak and believe it may be related to your solar installation, contact Venture Home right away to schedule a formal roof inspection.

> ⚠️ **Important:** Do not assume the source of the leak without an inspection — roof leaks can have many causes, and a proper inspection is needed to determine whether the installation is involved.

---

## Contact Venture Home Immediately

**Phone:** 800-203-4158
**Email:** Via the Venture Home app

**Why act quickly:**
- Prolonged water intrusion can damage your home's structure
- Can affect insulation and electrical systems
- Early intervention prevents costly repairs
- We want to rule out our work even if you're uncertain

> 💡 **Don't wait to reach out** — even if you're not certain solar is the cause, we'd rather come out and rule it out than have you dealing with a worsening leak.

---

## What to Expect

### Step 1: Schedule Inspection

Our team will schedule a technician to conduct a **thorough inspection** of:
- Roof penetrations from solar installation
- Flashing around mounting points
- Surrounding roof areas
- Attic space (if accessible)

**Timeline:** Typically 1-3 business days

### Step 2: Inspection Process

Our technician will:
- ✓ Examine all solar-related roof penetrations
- ✓ Check flashing integrity
- ✓ Look for signs of water intrusion paths
- ✓ Document findings with photos
- ✓ Determine if leak is installation-related

### Step 3: Determination and Resolution

**If leak IS related to solar installation:**
- Covered under Venture Home's **10-year workmanship warranty**
- We repair at **no cost to you**
- Includes fixing the leak and any related damage

**If leak is NOT related to solar installation:**
- Caused by aging roofing material
- Pre-existing flashing issues
- Other non-solar sources
- We provide documentation of findings
- You can proceed with your preferred roofing contractor

---

## Common Causes of Roof Leaks

### Solar-Related (Covered by Warranty)
- Improper flashing around mounts
- Sealant failure at penetrations
- Installation-related damage to roofing material

### Non-Solar Related (Not Covered)
- Aging or deteriorated roofing material
- Pre-existing flashing issues
- Storm damage to non-solar roof areas
- Clogged gutters causing water backup
- Normal wear and tear

### Can Be Either (Requires Inspection)
- Leak location near solar array
- Water stains near panels
- Multiple potential sources

---

## While You Wait for Inspection

### Temporary Measures

**You can do:**
- Place buckets to catch dripping water
- Move valuables away from leak area
- Document leak with photos/videos
- Note when leak started and conditions (rain, snow melt, etc.)

**Don't do:**
- Attempt to access roof yourself
- Try to "fix" flashing or seals
- Apply sealants or patches (can affect inspection)
- Ignore the leak hoping it stops
