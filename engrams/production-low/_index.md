# My Production Seems Low — What's Going On?

If you've noticed a higher-than-expected utility bill or feel like your solar system isn't performing the way it used to, you're not alone. This is one of the most common concerns we hear from customers, and **in most cases the system is actually working fine**.

Here's how to think through it.

---

## Your Bill Went Up, But Your System Is Performing Normally

The most common reason customers feel their production is low is a **higher utility bill** — but these are two different things.

**How it works:**
- Your solar system offsets a portion of your electricity usage
- Your utility bill reflects how much electricity you **pulled from the grid**
- If your **consumption increased**, your bill can rise even when your system is producing exactly as expected

### Before Assuming Something Is Wrong

**Consider whether anything in your home has changed:**
- New EV charger?
- New appliance (hot tub, pool pump, AC unit)?
- More people at home (work from home, guests, new family member)?
- Hot summer with heavy AC use?
- Electric heater use in winter?

> 💡 **Your solar system produces based on sunlight. Your bill depends on how much electricity you use.** If usage goes up, your bill goes up — even with perfect solar production.

---

## Seasonal Changes

**Solar production is naturally lower in fall and winter** due to:
- Shorter days (fewer sunlight hours)
- Lower sun angle (less direct sunlight)
- More cloud cover and weather

**Normal variation:**
- A system producing **40% less in December than it did in June is completely normal**
- Your system was sized for **annual** production, not monthly
- Summer surplus helps offset winter shortfalls

---

## How to Check if Your System Is Actually Underperforming

### Step 1: Check Your Monitoring App

Open your monitoring app and look at:
- **Recent production** — compare to same month last year
- **Daily production curves** — smooth bell curve on sunny days?
- **Error messages** — any alerts or warnings?

[[monitoring-by-manufacturer|Monitoring Your Solar System by Manufacturer]]

### Step 2: Compare Apples to Apples

**Don't compare:**
- ❌ This December to last June
- ❌ A cloudy week to a sunny week
- ❌ This month's bill to last month's bill (if usage changed)

**Do compare:**
- ✅ This December to last December
- ✅ Sunny days to sunny days
- ✅ Production (kWh) to expected production for time of year

### Step 3: Look for Red Flags

**System may have issues if:**
- Production is **zero** on sunny days
- Production dropped **suddenly** (not gradual seasonal decline)
- Monitoring app shows **error codes**
- One panel or section is **significantly lower** than others
- System has been **offline for days**

---

## Common Causes of Lower Production

### Normal / Expected
- Seasonal changes (shorter days in winter)
- Weather (cloudy days, rain, snow)
- Shading (growing trees, new construction)
- Aging (slight degradation over years — normal)

### Needs Attention
- System offline or error state
- Inverter failure
- Panel damage (cracks, delamination)
- Soiling (heavy dirt, bird droppings)
- Shading issues (new obstruction)

---

## What You Can Do

### Monitor for a Full Year

**Don't draw conclusions after a few weeks or months.** Solar performance varies significantly by season. Give it a full year of data before deciding your system is underperforming.

### Check Seasonal Expectations

Your system was designed for **annual production**. Monthly production will vary:
- **Summer:** 120-150% of average monthly
- **Winter:** 40-60% of average monthly
- **Spring/Fall:** 80-100% of average monthly

### When to Contact Us

**Contact Customer Success if:**
- Production is **zero** for multiple sunny days
- Monitoring app shows **error messages**
- Production suddenly **dropped significantly** (not gradual seasonal)
- System appears **offline**
- It's been a **full year** and production is consistently below expectations

| Question | Answer | Action |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Has your usage increased?** | Yes | That's likely the cause — monitor production, not just bill |
| **Is it winter?** | Yes | 40% lower is normal — compare to last winter |

| **Zero production on sunny days?** | Yes | **Contact us immediately** — system may be down |
| **Gradual decline over years?** | Yes | Slight degradation is normal (0.5% per year) |

- **Give it a full year** before concluding system is underperforming
- **Most "low production" concerns are actually normal variation**
