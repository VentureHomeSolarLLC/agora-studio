# What if My System Is Damaged?

Solar systems are built to withstand the elements, but severe weather events — hail, fallen trees, ice storms, high winds — can occasionally cause damage. Here's what to do if you believe your system has been damaged.

---

## Step 1: Don't Attempt Repairs Yourself

**Do not attempt to inspect, move, or repair any part of your solar system after a weather event.**

⚠️ **Safety Warning:** Damaged solar panels or wiring can present **electrical hazards** even when the system appears to be off.

**If you see visible damage:**
- Cracked panels
- Displaced racking
- Exposed wiring

→ **Keep the area clear and contact us immediately.**

---

## Step 2: Contact Venture Home

Reach out to our Customer Success team **as soon as you notice or suspect damage**.

**We will:**
- Dispatch a technician to inspect the system
- Document the damage thoroughly
- Provide you with a **formal damage report**

**Why this matters:** This documentation is exactly what your homeowner's insurance company will need to process a claim. We'll make sure you have everything required.

**Emergency Contact:** 800-203-4158

---

## Step 3: File a Claim with Your Homeowner's Insurance

Storm damage to a solar system is typically **covered under your homeowner's insurance policy**, as the system is part of your home's structure.

**How to file:**
1. Use the documentation Venture Home provides
2. Submit to your insurance company
3. If your insurer has questions about installation or equipment, our team can assist

**What Venture Home provides:**
- Formal damage report
- Photos of damage
- Equipment specifications
- Installation documentation
- Repair estimates

---

## Special Note for Lease and PPA Customers

Your finance provider carries insurance on the equipment they own.

**What to do:**
1. Contact **Venture Home** as soon as you identify damage
2. Contact your **finance provider** directly
3. **Do not attempt to arrange third-party repairs** — this could affect your agreement

**Find your provider's contact info:** [[finance-providers|Finance Provider Contact Information]]

## Common Types of Storm Damage

### Hail Damage
- Cracked or shattered panels
- May not be immediately visible
- Performance loss may indicate damage

### Wind Damage
- Displaced or missing panels
- Damaged racking/mounting
- Loose wiring

### Falling Trees/Debris
- Crushed panels
- Structural damage to mounting
- Potential roof damage

### Ice/Snow Storms
- Weight damage to panels (rare)
- Ice dam-related roof issues
- Wiring damage from ice expansion
