# Skill: Storm Damage Response

## Goal
Handle reports of storm damage safely, document thoroughly for insurance, coordinate inspections, and ensure proper repair process for both customer-owned and TPO systems.

## Priority Level
**EMERGENCY** — Same-day response required

## Safety First

⚠️ **CRITICAL:** Emphasize electrical hazard on every call. Customer must not touch damaged equipment.

## Information Gathering (Critical)

### Immediate Assessment

**Ask:**
1. "Are you and your family safe right now?"
2. "Do you see any exposed wiring or electrical hazards?"
3. "Is the damaged area accessible to children or pets?"
4. "When did the storm occur?"

**If electrical hazard suspected:**
- Advise customer to stay clear
- Suggest turning off main breaker if safe to do so
- Dispatch emergency technician if sparks/fire
- May need to involve fire department

### Damage Details

**Required information:**
- [ ] Storm type (hail, wind, falling tree, ice, etc.)
- [ ] Date/time of storm
- [ ] Visible damage description
- [ ] Customer-Owned or Lease/PPA system
- [ ] Photos (request immediately)
- [ ] System producing or offline?
- [ ] Any injuries or other property damage?

**If customer sends photos:**
- Review for severity
- Look for exposed wiring, cracked panels, displaced racking
- Note any roof damage
- Document for insurance file

## Customer-Owned Systems Process

### Step 1: Safety Confirmation

> "First, please make sure everyone stays away from the damaged area. Even if the system looks off, damaged solar equipment can still carry electrical current. Do not attempt to inspect or touch any panels or wiring."

### Step 2: Schedule Emergency Inspection

**Timeline:** Within 1-3 business days, sooner if safety hazard

**What to tell customer:**
> "I'm scheduling a technician to inspect the damage and document everything for your insurance claim. They'll arrive within [timeframe] and will call you [when] to confirm."

**Gather for scheduling:**
- Best days/times
- Any access restrictions
- Adult availability required
- Pets or other considerations

### Step 3: Insurance Documentation

**Explain process:**
> "Our technician will provide you with a formal damage report, photos, and repair estimate. This is exactly what your homeowner's insurance needs to process your claim. We'll make sure you have everything required."

**What we provide:**
- Detailed damage report
- High-resolution photos
- Equipment specifications
- Installation documentation
- Repair/replacement estimate
- Technical specifications for insurer

### Step 4: Follow-Up

**After inspection:**
- Confirm damage report delivered
- Offer to speak with insurance if they have technical questions
- Provide repair timeline estimate
- Schedule repairs after claim approval

## Lease/PPA Systems Process

### Critical Difference

**Finance provider owns equipment and carries insurance.** Customer should NOT arrange third-party repairs.

### Step 1: Safety + Dual Notification

> "Since you have a [lease/PPA], your finance provider actually owns the equipment and carries the insurance. I'll help coordinate with them, but you should also contact them directly. Do not arrange any repairs yourself — this needs to go through your finance provider."

### Step 2: Contact Finance Provider

**Provide customer:**
- Their finance provider contact info
- Suggested script for calling provider
- Copy of our damage report (we'll send to both)

**Finance providers to reference:**
- SunPower/SunStrong: (833) 514-1858
- Sunnova/SunStrong: (833) 514-1858
- Enfin: (833) 586-3677
- Etc. (see finance-providers concept)

### Step 3: Coordinate with Both Parties

**Our role:**
- Inspect and document damage
- Provide report to customer AND finance provider
- Be available for technical questions
- Coordinate repair timing with finance provider

**Customer's role:**
- Stay safe, keep area clear
- Contact finance provider
- Allow access for inspection
- Follow finance provider's process

### Step 4: Repair Coordination

- Work with finance provider's approved vendors
- Provide technical expertise if needed
- Ensure repairs meet our standards
- Document completion

## Documentation Requirements

### Damage Report Must Include

1. **Customer Information**
   - Name, address, contact info
   - System type and size
   - Installation date

2. **Storm Information**
   - Date and type of storm
   - Weather data if available
   - Duration and severity

3. **Damage Assessment**
   - List of all damaged components
   - Photos (minimum 10-15 angles)
   - Severity rating (minor/moderate/severe)
   - Safety hazards identified

4. **System Status**
   - Production before/after
   - Online/offline status
   - Any error codes

5. **Repair Estimate**
   - Parts needed
   - Labor estimate
   - Timeline for repairs
   - Alternative options if applicable

6. **Insurance Information**
   - Policy requirements met
   - Additional documentation available
   - Contact person at Venture Home for insurer questions

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Hail Damage

**Typical damage:** Cracked panels, may not be immediately visible
**Performance impact:** May show reduced production before visible damage
**Process:** 
- Inspection includes electrical testing
- Photos of all panels from multiple angles
- Production data analysis
- Often requires full panel replacement

### Scenario 2: Wind Damage

**Typical damage:** Displaced panels, damaged racking, loose wiring
**Safety concern:** High — displaced panels can fall
**Process:**
- Priority inspection (same day if possible)
- Structural integrity assessment
- Roof damage check
- Temporary securing if needed

### Scenario 3: Falling Tree/Debris

**Typical damage:** Crushed panels, structural mounting damage, potential roof damage
**Safety concern:** Very high
**Process:**
- Emergency response
- Roof integrity check
- Coordinate with roofers if needed
- Full system assessment (hidden damage common)

### Scenario 4: Ice/Snow Storm

**Typical damage:** Rare, but ice dams can affect roof mounting
**Process:**
- Inspection after safe access
- Structural assessment
- Wiring check for ice damage

## Insurance Coordination

### If Customer Asks for Help with Claim

**We can provide:**
- Technical specifications
- Installation details
- Equipment documentation
- Explanation of damage to adjuster
- Cost breakdowns

**We cannot:**
- File claim for customer
- Negotiate with insurer
- Guarantee coverage
- Provide legal advice

### Common Insurance Questions

**"Is this covered?"**
> "Storm damage to solar systems is typically covered under homeowner's insurance, but coverage varies by policy. Your insurer can confirm based on your specific policy terms."

**"How much will it cost?"**
> "Our damage report includes a detailed repair estimate. Your insurance adjuster will review this and determine coverage based on your policy."

**"Can I use any contractor?"**
> "For customer-owned systems, you can choose your contractor. For lease/PPA systems, your finance provider typically has approved vendors."

## Post-Repair Follow-Up

### Customer-Owned

- Confirm repairs completed
- Test system performance
- Provide warranty on repairs
- Update monitoring
- Follow up in 30 days

### Lease/PPA

- Confirm finance provider satisfied
- Verify repairs meet standards
- Ensure system performance restored
- Document completion for provider

## Escalation Triggers

**Escalate immediately to field operations:**
- Multiple homes damaged (storm event)
- Severe structural damage
- Injuries occurred
- Customer without power/heat
- Insurance claim denied (customer distressed)

**Escalate to warranty team:**
- Equipment failure vs. storm damage debate
- Manufacturer warranty questions
- Dispute over coverage

**Escalate to management:**
- Customer extremely dissatisfied
- Complex multi-party situation
- Significant business impact

## Key Phrases

**Safety emphasis:**
> "Even if the system looks off, damaged solar equipment can still carry electrical current. Please keep everyone away from the area."

**Documentation value:**
> "Our technician will document everything thoroughly. This report is exactly what your insurance needs."

**Lease/PPA reminder:**
> "Since your finance provider owns the equipment, they handle the insurance and repairs. I'll coordinate with them, but you should also contact them directly."

**Setting expectations:**
> "I know this is stressful. Here's exactly what will happen next..."
